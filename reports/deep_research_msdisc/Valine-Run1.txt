# Prebiotic Synthesis of Valine: Final s7 Report

## 1. Executive summary
This report is built **only from validated s5/s6 content**. Across the retained valine evidence bundle, **9 routes** were kept, but only **2 routes are yield-backed with numeric values plus citation and exact locator**. Both yield-backed routes are **spark-discharge atmospheric simulations from Ring & Miller (1984)**, with the strongest support for an **isobutane-fed discharge** route.

The validated s6 ranking places the routes as follows:
- **Best-supported route:** **VAL-R3**, spark discharge with isobutane feed, with normalized yield **0.01224 % carbon basis (inferred from 0.036% × 34.0/100)**; citation/locator: **Ring & Miller 1984, *Origins of Life and Evolution of the Biosphere*; Table I (isobutane experiment row)**.
- **Second yield-backed route:** **VAL-R4**, spark discharge with methane feed, with normalized yield **0.00204 % carbon basis for baseline CH4 run (inferred from 0.12% × 1.7/100)**; citation/locator: **Ring & Miller 1984, *Origins of Life and Evolution of the Biosphere*; Table I (CH4 baseline, CH4-A/B/C, and CH4-80° rows)**.

All other retained routes remain important for mechanistic or environmental context, but in the validated bundle they are **not yield-backed for valine specifically**. The Strecker sequence from **isobutyraldehyde → valine aminonitrile → valine** is chemically well grounded and source-supported, yet **no valine-specific numeric yield** was recovered. **Shock synthesis**, **ice/VUV photochemistry**, **2-ketoisovalerate amination**, **dry/high-pressure oligomerization**, and **hydrothermal degradation** are all retained, but mostly as qualitative routes, hypotheses, or sink/constraint processes rather than quantitatively established productive valine syntheses.

The consolidated network contains **9 precursor/intermediate species**, **9 reaction rows**, **6 direct target-forming routes**, **1 on-path intermediate route**, **2 sink/constraint routes**, and **4 inferred links**. The main unresolved gaps are that only **2/9 routes** are yield-backed, and **hydrothermal, meteoritic, mineral/metal, and ice-UV families remain weakly quantified or source-access-limited**.

## 3. Per-route narrative

### VAL-R3 — Spark discharge with isobutane feed
This is the strongest route in the validated bundle because it is one of only two routes with a numeric valine yield carried through s6. The route uses **isobutane + N2 + NH3 + H2O** in a spark-discharge system with **tungsten electrodes**, an **NH4Cl aqueous trap**, and **post-run 3 M HCl hydrolysis**. Reported conditions are `~25 °C bulk flask; localized spark much hotter`, `N2 80 torr; hydrocarbon 150 torr; water vapor ~25 torr`, and `pH 8.7 (0.05 M NH4Cl adjusted with ~1 mL 1 M NaOH)`. The exact yield text retained is: `valine/glycine mole ratio 34.0; glycine yield 0.036% based on carbon; inferred valine yield ≈0.01224% based on carbon`, normalized as `0.01224 % carbon basis (inferred from 0.036% × 34.0/100)`, from **Ring & Miller 1984, *Origins of Life and Evolution of the Biosphere*; Table I (isobutane experiment row)**. The validated s6 note adds that the authors interpret the relatively high valine yield with isobutane as arising via oxidation to **isobutyraldehyde hydrate** followed by **Strecker synthesis**.

### VAL-R4 — Spark discharge with methane feed
This is the second yield-backed route. It uses **CH4 + N2 + NH3 + H2O** with **electric spark**, **tungsten electrodes**, **0.05 M NH4Cl solution**, and **post-run 3 M HCl hydrolysis**. Conditions retained are `25 °C bulk flask in CH4-A/B/C; ~80 °C in CH4-80° experiment`, `CH4 300 torr; N2 80 torr; NH3 0.02, 0.5, or 2.5 torr; water vapor ~25 torr at 25 °C and ~350 torr at 80 °C`, and `baseline NH4Cl solution adjusted to pH 8.7`. The exact yield text is `baseline methane experiment: valine/glycine ratio 1.7; glycine yield 0.12% based on carbon; inferred valine yield ≈0.00204% based on carbon. CH4-A/B/C relative valine ratios 1.0, 2.7, 10.2. CH4-80° relative valine ratio 0.061 with glycine yield 1.02%.`, normalized as `0.00204 % carbon basis for baseline CH4 run (inferred from 0.12% × 1.7/100)`, from **Ring & Miller 1984, *Origins of Life and Evolution of the Biosphere*; Table I (CH4 baseline, CH4-A/B/C, and CH4-80° rows)**. In the validated ranking, this route is weaker than VAL-R3 because valine is minor in methane-only spark runs relative to isobutane-fed runs.

### VAL-R1 — Strecker formation of valine aminonitrile from isobutyraldehyde
This route maps the first half of a classical Strecker sequence: **isobutyraldehyde + NH3/NH4+ + HCN/CN− → valine aminonitrile**. The validated table gives `aqueous or mixed aqueous` medium, `near-neutral to mildly basic/acidic window not valine-specifically resolved`, and no reported T or P. The environmental framing retained is `cyanide-bearing pond, evaporative lagoon, meteoritic parent-body aqueous setting, or impact-generated aqueous niche`. The route is **source-supported**, but the yield field is explicitly `yield not reported (source: VAL-R1-primary-unrecovered; locations checked: full text + SI unavailable in workspace/source-list context)`. The validated synthesis memo ranks the Strecker sequence as chemically well grounded but not quantitatively established for valine in the accepted bundle.

### VAL-R2 — Hydrolysis of valine aminonitrile to valine
This is the second half of the Strecker sequence: **valine aminonitrile + H2O → valine**. The retained conditions are `aqueous`, `105 °C (laboratory hydrolysis analogue)`, `acidic during laboratory hydrolysis; prebiotic hydrolysis unresolved`, and `24 h (laboratory hydrolysis analogue)`. The route is treated as a generic, source-supported Strecker conversion, but again the yield is explicitly `yield not reported (source: VAL-R2-primary-unrecovered; locations checked: full text + SI unavailable in workspace/source-list context)`. In the validated bundle, this route remains plausible but quantitatively unresolved under prebiotic conditions.

### VAL-R5 — Shock synthesis in reduced gas mixture
This route uses **CH4 + C2H6 + NH3 + H2O** under **shock wave energy** in a `gas mixture / primitive-environment simulation`. The retained conditions are only qualitative: `transient high-T shock; exact value not extracted` and `transient high-P shock; exact value not extracted`. The environmental interpretation is `impact/shock chemistry in primitive atmosphere or atmosphere-ocean interface`. The validated note states that secondary sources say valine was among products, but no numeric valine yield was recovered. Accordingly, the yield remains `yield not reported (source: Bar-Nun-1970-primary-unrecovered; locations checked: full text unavailable; secondary summary in Ring & Miller 1984 + source-list context)`.

### VAL-R6 — VUV photochemistry of mixed ices followed by hydrolysis
This route represents **surface-UV / ice photochemistry** in mixed ices containing **H2O, CO, CO2, CH3OH, CH4, NH3**, followed by hydrolysis. Retained conditions are `10–80 K irradiation; warm-up to room temperature; hydrolysis 105 °C for 24 h`, `static vacuum ~10^-5 mbar during residue handling`, and `2.2 analytical buffer; 6 M HCl hydrolysis in workflow`, with `VUV photons; acid hydrolysis; cation-exchange LC detection`. The environment is `interstellar/cometary ice analogues`. The validated route table preserves the statement that `amino acids formed in all experiments; total amino acids in hydrolysed residues ranged from 2.8 to 59.0 nmol across experiments; valine included in analytical standard, but valine-specific quantity not extracted from accessible PDF`. Therefore the valine yield is explicitly `yield not reported (source: Nuevo-2008; locations checked: full text Table 1/text + no SI recovered; total amino acids 2.8–59.0 nmol reported, but valine-specific quantity not recovered)`. The validated s6 synthesis also flags a contradiction: route-family support is robust, but **valine-specific support is weaker and contested**.

### VAL-R7 — α-ketoacid amination from 2-ketoisovalerate
This route is framed as **2-ketoisovalerate + nitrogen donor/reductant → valine** in an `aqueous` setting, with `cyanate in general α-ketoacid route; possible reducing equivalents/dinucleotide-like catalysts discussed`. The environment is `metabolism-like aqueous network / moderate pond or non-extreme hydrothermal fringe`. However, T, P, and pH are all `not reported`, and the yield is explicitly `yield not reported (source: VAL-R7-direct-prebiotic-demo-unrecovered; locations checked: full text + SI/source-list context for Liu 2022 and related route papers)`. The validated s6 synthesis treats this route as **mechanistically attractive but still hypothesis/inference**, not a direct demonstrated enzyme-free prebiotic valine route.

### VAL-R8 — Dry/high-pressure oligomerization of valine
This route is retained because it is experimentally relevant to valine’s fate, but the validated bundle treats it as a **sink/assembly route from valine**, not a productive route to free valine. Conditions are `150–200 °C`, `50–150 MPa`, `not applicable / non-aqueous`, `48–192 h`, and `non-aqueous solid-state / dehydrating`, in `deep prebiotic marine sediments undergoing diagenesis`. The route table explicitly gives `yield not reported` for the s4y-compliant route row because exact page-level locator verification for numeric values was not available under the task rules. At the network level, however, the validated note records that `linear peptide yields 0.19–0.36% at 48 h, decreasing to 0.07–0.16% at 192 h; at 200 °C and 100 MPa for 192 h, NH3 yield up to 20% and glycine yield up to 0.015%; higher pressure preserved more residual valine and peptides`. The validated contradictions table explicitly states that VAL-R8 should be interpreted separately from productive synthesis routes.

### VAL-R9 — Hydrothermal degradation of valine in subcritical water
This route is also a **constraint/sink**, not a productive synthesis route. Conditions are `290–330 °C`, `subcritical water conditions; exact P not extracted`, `aqueous subcritical water`, and `hot compressed water`, in a `hydrothermal / high-temperature aqueous system`. The validated route row states `degradation kinetics reported; numeric rate constants not extracted because primary paper was not accessed`, and the yield is explicitly `yield not reported (source: Kobayashi-2010-primary-unrecovered; locations checked: source-list context/review summary; full text + SI not recovered)`. In the validated ranking, this route is retained mainly to constrain environmental plausibility by showing that many hydrothermal settings may destroy free valine rather than accumulate it.

## 5. Mineral catalysts and surfaces
The validated route table contains limited direct mineral catalysis for productive valine synthesis, but the retained source and precursor bundle does preserve several mineral/surface contexts:

- For the **ice/VUV route (VAL-R6)**, the route table lists **`MgF2 or Cu substrates depending on experiment`** under `mineral_surface`, with the route occurring in `cryogenic ice then residue then aqueous hydrolysate` under `interstellar/cometary ice analogues`.
- The deduplicated retained source list includes direct mineral/surface entries such as:
  - **`Infrared spectra of valine adsorbed on Cu-montmorillonite` (1972, American Mineralogist)**, described in the retained relevance note as showing `IR evidence for bidentate chelate and monodentate Cu-valine complexes and pH dependence`.
  - **`Possible selective adsorption of enantiomers by Na-montmorillonite ...` (1982, NASA NTRS)**, described as a direct valine hit where `racemic valine and norvaline were incubated with Na-montmorillonite and adsorption/enantiomer differences assessed`.
  - General clay-catalyzed condensation/polymerization background sources such as **`The mechanism of clay catalyzed polymerization of amino acid ...` (1976)** and **`The polymerization of amino acid adenylates on sodium-montmorillonite ...` (1988)**.
  - Broader Fe/S plausibility context from **`On the prebiotic role of iron and sulfur` (2001)**.

Within the validated s6 synthesis, these mineral and surface sources are not elevated to productive, yield-backed valine-forming routes. Instead, they support **adsorption, complexation, chiral selection, or condensation context**, while the explicit gap statement says **mineral/metal families remain weakly quantified or source-access-limited**.

## 6. Experimental evidence and environmental context
### Strongest experimental evidence
The strongest direct experimental evidence in the validated bundle is the **spark-discharge family**, especially **isobutane-fed discharge**. Both yield-backed routes come from **Ring & Miller (1984)** and include explicit conditions, quantitative inference, and exact table locators. The validated ranking therefore places **VAL-R3** first and **VAL-R4** second.

### Strecker environmental context
The Strecker sequence is mapped onto `cyanide-bearing pond, evaporative lagoon, meteoritic parent-body aqueous setting, or impact-generated aqueous niche`. This makes it environmentally plausible in several prebiotic settings, but the accepted bundle does not recover valine-specific numeric yields for either aminonitrile formation or hydrolysis.

### Shock context
The shock route is retained as `impact/shock chemistry in primitive atmosphere or atmosphere-ocean interface`. It is experimentally suggestive but quantitatively unresolved for valine in the validated bundle.

### Ice/UV context
The ice photochemistry route is retained for `interstellar/cometary ice analogues`. The validated evidence supports general amino-acid formation strongly, but valine-specific assignment remains weaker. The route therefore contributes environmental plausibility for extraterrestrial or nebular precursor chemistry, but not a quantified valine synthesis yield.

### Dry high-pressure and hydrothermal context
The validated bundle treats **dry/high-pressure oligomerization** and **hydrothermal degradation** mainly as **post-formation fate constraints**. Dry, pressurized, water-poor settings can oligomerize valine and generate decomposition products, while hot compressed water can degrade valine. These routes therefore matter for environmental survivability and accumulation, not just synthesis.

### Meteoritic context
The validated gaps table preserves the open question: `What fraction of meteoritic valine reflects parent-body Strecker synthesis versus terrestrial contamination or analytical ambiguity?` The deduplicated source list contains many meteoritic records, but the validated synthesis explicitly notes that meteoritic family coverage remains weakly quantified or source-access-limited for this task.

## 7. Open questions and debates
The validated s6 gaps/contradictions/open-questions table identifies the following unresolved issues:

### Gaps
- `Only 2 of 9 routes are yield-backed with numeric values plus citation/locator in s4y (VAL-R3, VAL-R4).`
- `Hydrothermal, meteoritic, mineral/metal, and ice-UV families remain weakly quantified or source-access-limited; most retained as yield not reported or contested.`

### Contradictions
- `VAL-R8 is a downstream sink/assembly route from valine, not a synthesis route to free valine, yet appears in the same route bundle; it should be interpreted separately from productive routes.`
- `Ice-UV route retains valine as contested/weakly assigned in accessible extraction, while general amino-acid formation is robust; valine-specific support is therefore weaker than route-family support.`

### Open questions
- `Can enzyme-free 2-ketoisovalerate amination produce valine directly under plausible prebiotic conditions with measurable yield?`
- `Are branched aldehydes such as isobutyraldehyde sufficiently available in relevant prebiotic environments to make Strecker valine competitive with spark-discharge production?`
- `Do hydrothermal settings ever net-produce valine, or are they predominantly destructive for free valine once formed?`
- `What fraction of meteoritic valine reflects parent-body Strecker synthesis versus terrestrial contamination or analytical ambiguity?`
