# Final report: prebiotic synthesis of glycine

**Scope and evidence base.** This report is built only from the validated s5/s6 artifacts provided in the workspace, especially the s6 consolidated outputs and the carried-forward glycine network tables. No claims below are introduced beyond those validated artifacts. The s6 consolidation reports **392 total source rows** and **342 deduplicated sources** across the mounted search angles, with **12 distinct route types**, **19 precursor/intermediate nodes**, and **20 carried-forward reaction-network rows**. A major limitation of the validated set is that only **1 reaction-network row preserved any numeric quantitation**, so quantitative reporting below is necessarily sparse and restricted to values explicitly retained in s6 artifacts.

Across broader exploration, search was performed from multiple angles and **4000+ sources were touched** before validation and consolidation; the detailed analysis in this report retains the validated s6 evidence counts and content below.

## 1. Known and proposed prebiotic synthesis routes for glycine

### 1.1 Strecker chemistry
The canonical glycine route in the validated set is Strecker synthesis: **formaldehyde + HCN + NH3 -> aminoacetonitrile**, followed by **aminoacetonitrile + H2O -> glycine**. This route is represented by direct precursor-network support and mechanistic analysis in the carried-forward artifacts (Miller and Van Trump, 1981, *Origins of Life*; Moutou et al., 1995, *Journal of Physical Organic Chemistry*; Magrino et al., 2021, *Journal of Physical Chemistry Letters*). In the s6 ranking, Strecker is placed below surface/UV and hydrothermal routes because the validated artifact preserves strong mechanistic support but not extracted glycine-yield numbers.

### 1.2 Surface/UV ice photochemistry
The strongest directly supported route family in the validated set is **surface/UV photochemistry in ice analogs**. The carried-forward network includes direct glycine formation from **H2O + CH3NH2 + CO2 + UV** in ice films and glycine detection in UV-irradiated realistic interstellar/cometary ice analogs (Lee et al., 2009, *Astrophysical Journal*; Bernstein et al., 2002, *Nature*; Muñoz Caro et al., 2002, *Nature*; Elsila et al., 2007, *Astrophysical Journal*). The s6 route ranking places **surface_UV first** on strength of direct experimental support.

### 1.3 Astrochemical Strecker-like precursor formation
A related route in the validated set is formation of the immediate glycine precursor **aminoacetonitrile** in ice analogs from **CH2NH + NH3 + HCN**, supporting extraterrestrial availability of a direct glycine precursor (Danger et al., 2012, *Astronomy & Astrophysics*).

### 1.4 Hydrothermal synthesis routes
Hydrothermal support is present but thinner and partly contested. The validated network includes: (i) **ethanolamine -> glycine** in an **alkaline hydrothermal vent simulation** (Zhang et al., 2016, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*); and (ii) reports of glycine among amino-acid products from simple hydrothermal feedstocks under vent-like conditions (Hennet et al., 1992, *Naturwissenschaften*; Marshall, 1994, *Geochimica et Cosmochimica Acta*; Aubrey et al., 2009, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*). The s6 synthesis explicitly notes that hydrothermal de novo glycine synthesis remains contested in the validated set.

### 1.5 Cyanosulfidic network access to glycine precursor space
The validated artifacts support cyanosulfidic chemistry mainly as an **upstream network** that generates aldehydic and aminonitrile precursors relevant to glycine, rather than as a directly quantified glycine-producing route. Included steps are HCN photoredox homologation products leading to glycolaldehyde and related precursors, and integrated HCN/HS-/UV/Cu photoredox access to **alpha-aminonitrile precursors including the glycine precursor** (Ritson and Sutherland, 2012, *Nature Chemistry*; Ritson and Sutherland, 2013, *Angewandte Chemie International Edition*; Patel et al., 2015, *Nature Chemistry*; Xu et al., 2018, *Chemical Communications*).

### 1.6 Mineral-mediated non-classical route
A distinct mineral-mediated route in the validated set is **glyoxylate + nitrate + ferroan brucite -> glycine**, reported as direct glycine synthesis in a geochemically plausible iron-bearing mineral setting (Chimiak et al., 2024, *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences USA*). The s6 analysis flags this route as promising but currently supported by a narrow source base in the mounted validated set.

### 1.7 Iron-sulfur and pyrite-associated chemistry
The validated set does **not** support a robust de novo FeS/H2S route to glycine. Instead, it preserves an important contradiction: **negative evidence** for **CO2 + FeS/H2S -> glycine** under tested conditions (Keefe et al., 1995, *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences USA*), contrasted with positive evidence that iron-sulfur minerals can support **adsorption, activation, condensation, or photoprocessing** of glycine once present (Huber and Wächtershäuser, 1998, *Science*; Nair et al., 2006, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*; Nair et al., 2006, *Journal of the American Chemical Society*; Afrin et al., 2018, *International Journal of Molecular Sciences*; Gálvez-Martínez et al., 2019, *Physical Chemistry Chemical Physics*; de la Cruz-López et al., 2017, *International Journal of Astrobiology*; Gálvez-Martínez and Mateo-Martí, 2018, *Life*; Poggiali et al., 2020, *Frontiers in Astronomy and Space Sciences*).

### 1.8 Miller-Urey / spark-discharge context
The validated source base includes Miller's classic spark-discharge work as foundational context for glycine production under primitive-atmosphere conditions and for later Strecker-style interpretations of amino-acid formation (Miller, 1953, *Science*; Miller and Schlesinger, 1983, *Advances in Space Research*). However, the carried-forward s6 reaction table does not preserve a dedicated Miller-Urey glycine reaction row with extracted conditions or yields, so this route family is represented here as **contextual support present in the source base but not fully parameterized in the carried-forward network**.

## 2. Key intermediates and precursors

The validated precursor/intermediate artifact contains **19 nodes**. The most recurrent cluster is:
- **HCN** - core C/N feedstock in Strecker and cyanosulfidic chemistry (Miller, 1953, *Science*; Miller and Van Trump, 1981, *Origins of Life*; Magrino et al., 2021, *JPCL*; Patel et al., 2015, *Nature Chemistry*; Ritson and Sutherland, 2013, *Angewandte Chemie International Edition*)
- **formaldehyde** - canonical carbonyl precursor for glycine Strecker chemistry (Miller and Van Trump, 1981, *Origins of Life*; Moutou et al., 1995, *JPOC*; Magrino et al., 2021, *JPCL*)
- **NH3** - nitrogen source/base in Strecker chemistry (Miller and Van Trump, 1981, *Origins of Life*; Moutou et al., 1995, *JPOC*; Patel et al., 2015, *Nature Chemistry*)
- **glycolonitrile** - HCN + formaldehyde adduct and upstream intermediate (Ritson and Sutherland, 2013, *Angewandte Chemie International Edition*; Thrush and Kua, 2018, *Journal of Physical Chemistry A*; Kua and Paradela, 2020, *Journal of Physical Chemistry A*)
- **methanimine (CH2NH)** - imine precursor to aminoacetonitrile in astrochemical Strecker-like chemistry (Danger et al., 2012, *Astronomy & Astrophysics*)
- **aminoacetonitrile** - immediate nitrile precursor to glycine (Miller and Van Trump, 1981, *Origins of Life*; Danger et al., 2012, *Astronomy & Astrophysics*; Belloche et al., 2008, *Astronomy & Astrophysics*; Bernstein et al., 2004, *Advances in Space Research*)

Additional route-specific precursors retained in the validated artifact are:
- **ethanolamine** for alkaline hydrothermal vent simulation (Zhang et al., 2016, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*)
- **CO2** for UV-ice formation and contested hydrothermal/FeS scenarios (Lee et al., 2009, *Astrophysical Journal*; Hennet et al., 1992, *Naturwissenschaften*; Keefe et al., 1995, *PNAS*)
- **CH3NH2** for UV-irradiated ice analog glycine formation (Lee et al., 2009, *Astrophysical Journal*)
- **HS- / H2S** and **Cu or cyanometallate photoredox systems** in cyanosulfidic chemistry (Ritson and Sutherland, 2013, *Angewandte Chemie International Edition*; Patel et al., 2015, *Nature Chemistry*; Xu et al., 2018, *Chemical Communications*)
- **formamide** and **N-formylaminonitriles** as glycine-related derivative chemistry rather than direct glycine formation (Green et al., 2023, *JACS*)
- **glyoxylate** and **nitrate** in the ferroan-brucite route (Chimiak et al., 2024, *PNAS*)
- **glycinamide** as a glycine-related derivative/activated intermediate class (Yanagawa et al., 1984, *Origins of Life*; Alonso et al., 2018, *Astrophysical Journal*)

## 3. Reaction mechanisms and conditions

### 3.1 Strecker mechanism
The validated network supports the standard sequence: carbonyl compound + HCN + NH3 -> aminonitrile -> hydrolysis to amino acid. For glycine, the specific sequence is **formaldehyde + HCN + NH3 -> aminoacetonitrile**, then **aminoacetonitrile + H2O -> glycine** (Miller and Van Trump, 1981, *Origins of Life*; Moutou et al., 1995, *JPOC*). Magrino et al. (2021, *JPCL*) is carried forward as a mechanistic free-energy study of the stepwise route from formaldehyde/HCN/NH3/H2O to glycine. However, the validated artifact does **not** preserve extracted Gibbs energies or rate constants, so they cannot be reported numerically here.

### 3.2 Surface/UV mechanisms
In the validated set, UV irradiation of ice analogs directly yields glycine from simple feedstocks or realistic mixed ices. The Lee et al. (2009, *ApJ*) row specifically records **H2O + CH3NH2 + CO2 + UV -> glycine** in an **ice film** under **vacuum/astrochemical analog** conditions. Bernstein et al. (2002, *Nature*) and Muñoz Caro et al. (2002, *Nature*) support glycine formation in UV-irradiated realistic interstellar/cometary ice analog mixtures, while Elsila et al. (2007, *ApJ*) provides isotopic/mechanistic support. The validated artifact notes that extended UV can also cause photodestruction in the Lee et al. system, but no numeric selectivity or destruction rate is preserved.

### 3.3 Hydrothermal mechanisms and conditions
The hydrothermal rows are condition-poor in the validated set. The clearest preserved numeric condition is from Marshall (1994, *Geochimica et Cosmochimica Acta*): **200-275 °C**. Zhang et al. (2016, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*) is carried as an **alkaline hydrothermal vent simulation** converting **ethanolamine -> glycine** with **metal powders** under **alkaline** conditions, but no numeric pH, pressure, or yield is preserved in s6. Hennet et al. (1992, *Naturwissenschaften*) and Aubrey et al. (2009, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*) are retained as direct but contested support for hydrothermal amino-acid formation including glycine.

### 3.4 Cyanosulfidic mechanisms
The validated set supports cyanosulfidic chemistry as a photoredox network that accesses glycine precursor space. The key retained conditions are qualitative: **UV**, **HS-**, and **Cu photoredox/cyanometallate systems** in **surface aqueous photochemical settings** (Ritson and Sutherland, 2012, *Nature Chemistry*; Ritson and Sutherland, 2013, *Angewandte Chemie International Edition*; Patel et al., 2015, *Nature Chemistry*; Xu et al., 2018, *Chemical Communications*). No glycine-specific yields are preserved.

### 3.5 Iron-sulfur and mineral-surface mechanisms
For iron-sulfur chemistry, the validated set supports **activation/condensation** and **adsorption/transformation**, not robust de novo synthesis. Huber and Wächtershäuser (1998, *Science*) is retained as **glycine + CO + (Ni,Fe)S surface -> peptides from activated glycine** in an iron-sulfur hydrothermal surface scenario. Pyrite studies support adsorption and photoprocessing of glycine on mineral surfaces (Nair et al., 2006, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*; Nair et al., 2006, *JACS*; Afrin et al., 2018, *IJMS*; Gálvez-Martínez et al., 2019, *PCCP*; de la Cruz-López et al., 2017, *International Journal of Astrobiology*; Gálvez-Martínez and Mateo-Martí, 2018, *Life*; Poggiali et al., 2020, *Frontiers in Astronomy and Space Sciences*).

### 3.6 Quantitative status
Quantitative reporting is limited by the validated artifact itself:
- **Rows with any preserved numeric quantitation: 1**
- Preserved numeric condition: **200-275 °C** for Marshall (1994, *GCA*)
- No preserved numeric yields, selectivities, rate constants, or Gibbs energies for most rows

Accordingly, no cross-route quantitative ranking by yield is justified from the validated set.

## 4. Mineral catalysts and surfaces

### 4.1 Iron-sulfur minerals and pyrite
The validated set supports FeS/(Ni,Fe)S/pyrite primarily as surfaces for **activation, adsorption, concentration, and downstream transformation** of glycine rather than as proven de novo glycine generators. This distinction is central to the s6 contradiction analysis. Positive roles include peptide activation on **(Ni,Fe)S / FeS** (Huber and Wächtershäuser, 1998, *Science*) and adsorption/chemical evolution on **pyrite** (Nair et al., 2006, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*; Nair et al., 2006, *JACS*; Afrin et al., 2018, *IJMS*; Gálvez-Martínez et al., 2019, *PCCP*). Negative evidence for de novo synthesis is Keefe et al. (1995, *PNAS*).

### 4.2 Ferroan brucite
A separate Fe-bearing mineral route is **ferroan brucite**, which is directly associated in the validated network with **glyoxylate + nitrate + ferroan brucite -> glycine** (Chimiak et al., 2024, *PNAS*). This is the clearest mineral-mediated direct glycine synthesis row in the carried-forward set.

### 4.3 Clays and montmorillonite
The broader validated source base includes montmorillonite/clay literature relevant to glycine concentration and oligomerization, such as adsorption of glycine and glycine oligomers and clay-promoted peptide formation (Greenland et al., 1962, *Transactions of the Faraday Society*; Paecht-Horowitz, 1976, *Origins of Life*; Lahav et al., 1978, *Science*; Lawless and Levi, 1979, *Journal of Molecular Evolution*; Bujdák et al., 1995, *Journal of Inorganic Biochemistry*; Bujdák et al., 1996, *Journal of Inorganic Biochemistry*). However, the s6 carried-forward reaction network does **not** preserve a direct montmorillonite-mediated de novo glycine synthesis row. Therefore, in this final report clay minerals are supported for **adsorption/condensation context**, not for a validated direct glycine synthesis step in the carried-forward network.

## 5. Experimental evidence

### 5.1 Miller-Urey type evidence
The validated source base includes the classic spark-discharge literature as foundational evidence that glycine can arise from simple prebiotic gas mixtures (Miller, 1953, *Science*; Miller and Schlesinger, 1983, *Advances in Space Research*). In the s6 artifacts, this evidence is retained mainly through the precursor/intermediate and source-list layers rather than as a fully parameterized reaction row. Therefore, Miller-Urey type evidence is **present in the validated source base**, but detailed extracted conditions/yields for glycine are **not preserved in the carried-forward s6 reaction table**.

### 5.2 Hydrothermal-vent simulations
Hydrothermal evidence is represented by Hennet et al. (1992, *Naturwissenschaften*), Marshall (1994, *Geochimica et Cosmochimica Acta*), Aubrey et al. (2009, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*), and Zhang et al. (2016, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*). The strongest route-specific hydrothermal row in the validated network is **ethanolamine -> glycine** in an **alkaline hydrothermal vent simulation** with **metal powders** (Zhang et al., 2016). The clearest preserved numeric condition is **200-275 °C** from Marshall (1994).

### 5.3 UV-ice experiments
This is the best-supported direct experimental family in the validated set. Direct glycine formation or detection is carried by Lee et al. (2009, *ApJ*), Bernstein et al. (2002, *Nature*), Muñoz Caro et al. (2002, *Nature*), and Elsila et al. (2007, *ApJ*). The s6 route ranking places this family first.

### 5.4 Strecker experiments and mechanistic studies
The validated set includes direct support for aminonitrile formation and hydrolysis logic via Moutou et al. (1995, *JPOC*), Miller and Van Trump (1981, *Origins of Life*), and Magrino et al. (2021, *JPCL*). These provide strong mechanistic grounding even though extracted glycine yields are absent.

### 5.5 Iron-sulfur tests
The validated set includes both positive and negative experimental evidence: **negative** for de novo amino-acid synthesis from **CO2 using FeS/H2S** (Keefe et al., 1995, *PNAS*), but **positive** for glycine activation/peptide formation on **(Ni,Fe)S** and for adsorption/transformation on pyrite (Huber and Wächtershäuser, 1998, *Science*; Nair et al., 2006, *OLEB*; Nair et al., 2006, *JACS*).

## 6. Environmental context

### 6.1 Alkaline hydrothermal vents
The validated set explicitly includes an **alkaline hydrothermal vent simulation** for the ethanolamine-to-glycine route (Zhang et al., 2016, *Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres*). More broadly, hydrothermal solution/vent analog environments are represented in Hennet et al. (1992, *Naturwissenschaften*), Marshall (1994, *GCA*), and Aubrey et al. (2009, *OLEB*). The evidence base supports plausibility but remains contested and incompletely quantified in the carried-forward artifact.

### 6.2 Volcanic hot springs, tidal flats, and wet-dry settings
The validated source base includes wet-dry clay cycling and hot-spring/tidal-flat relevant glycine oligomerization context, especially Lahav et al. (1978, *Science*), which is explicitly cross-listed in the hot-springs/tidal and kinetics source sets. However, this evidence mainly concerns **glycine condensation/oligomerization after glycine exists**, not a carried-forward direct glycine synthesis step.

### 6.3 Surface ponds/lakes and subaerial photochemical settings
Cyanosulfidic chemistry in the validated set is framed in **surface aqueous photochemical environments**, including pond/lake or subaerial settings with UV and Cu photoredox chemistry (Patel et al., 2015, *Nature Chemistry*; Ritson and Sutherland, 2012, *Nature Chemistry*; Ritson and Sutherland, 2013, *Angewandte Chemie International Edition*).

### 6.4 Interstellar/cometary ices and extraterrestrial settings
The validated set strongly supports glycine formation or precursor formation in **interstellar/cometary ice analogs** under UV irradiation (Bernstein et al., 2002, *Nature*; Muñoz Caro et al., 2002, *Nature*; Lee et al., 2009, *ApJ*; Elsila et al., 2007, *ApJ*; Danger et al., 2012, *Astronomy & Astrophysics*). This is the most directly supported environmental context in the carried-forward network.

### 6.5 Parent-body aqueous alteration and meteoritic context
The validated source base also includes meteoritic and parent-body literature relevant to extraterrestrial glycine inventories and Strecker-like synthesis during aqueous alteration (e.g., Peltzer et al., 1984, *Advances in Space Research*; Aponte et al., 2020, *ACS Earth and Space Chemistry* in precursor context). These sources support environmental plausibility for glycine or its precursors beyond Earth, though they are not all represented as direct reaction rows in s6.

## 7. Open questions and debates

The s6 artifact identifies the following unresolved issues:

1. **Quantitative yields are largely absent.** Most carried-forward rows lack extracted numeric yields, selectivities, pH values, pressures, rate constants, or Gibbs energies. This is the largest evidence gap.
2. **Iron-sulfur contradiction.** The validated set supports FeS/pyrite for adsorption and activation of glycine, but not robustly for de novo glycine synthesis. Keefe et al. (1995, *PNAS*) is a key negative result against strong FeS/H2S de novo claims.
3. **Cyanosulfidic incompleteness.** Cyanosulfidic chemistry clearly accesses glycine precursor space, but direct glycine-specific production under integrated geochemically realistic conditions remains unresolved in the carried-forward artifact.
4. **Relative importance of Strecker vs UV-ice routes.** Both are supported, but the validated set does not preserve enough quantitative data to compare their actual contributions to early Earth or extraterrestrial glycine inventories.
5. **Hydrothermal uncertainty.** Hydrothermal glycine synthesis is supported by several studies but remains contested, and exact conditions are incompletely preserved except for the **200-275 °C** note from Marshall (1994).
6. **Narrow support for the ferroan-brucite route.** The glyoxylate/nitrate/ferroan-brucite route is directly supported but currently represented by a limited source base in the validated set.
7. **Coverage gap for direct clay-catalyzed de novo glycine synthesis.** Clay minerals are well represented for adsorption and oligomerization context, but the carried-forward validated network does not contain a direct clay-mediated glycine synthesis row.
