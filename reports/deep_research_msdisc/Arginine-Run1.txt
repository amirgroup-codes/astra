# Prebiotic synthesis report for arginine

## 1. Executive summary
Using only the existing s6 consolidated bundle as the best-available validated basis, the evidence base for prebiotic arginine is dominated not by productive synthesis with quantified yield, but by **absence/non-detection**, **underconstrained plausibility**, and **destruction under hydrothermal conditions**. The s6 summary reports **10 total retained routes**, of which **0 are yield-backed**, **5 are non-yield routes**, and **5 are explicit arginine-not-detected routes** (`arginine_s6_summary.json`). No route in the retained bundle carries a numeric arginine yield with citation and exact locator; this is an explicit gap in the upstream analysis (`arginine_s6_summary.json`; `arginine_s6_final_submission.md`).

The strongest positive-but-underconstrained route retained is **ornithine + cyanamide -> arginine** (`ARG_R1`), cited to Longo et al. 2020 (*PNAS*) as contextual support, but the route is explicitly marked as inferred and as having **"yield not reported"** because no exact primary table/figure/page could be inspected in the recovered source context (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). A second strong network edge is not synthetic but degradative: **arginine + hot water -> ornithine (+ additional products)** under hydrothermal conditions (`ARG_R2`), indicating that hydrothermal settings may act more as arginine sinks than sources in the retained bundle (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`).

The bundle also preserves repeated negative evidence: arginine is **not detected** in canonical Miller-Urey spark discharge (`ARG_R3`), H2S-rich Miller archive discharge (`ARG_R4`), interstellar/cometary ice UV photolysis (`ARG_R5`), meteoritic inventories (`ARG_R6`), and mixed-valence iron oxyhydroxide hydrothermal/mineral-gradient systems (`ARG_R10`) (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). This repeated absence is therefore a central result, not a side note.

Overall conclusion from the retained s6 evidence: arginine is chemically conceivable in prebiotic networks involving ornithine/cyanamide or biochemical analogs through citrulline/argininosuccinate, but **the best-supported observations in this bundle are non-detection and degradation rather than demonstrated abiotic accumulation of free arginine**.

## 3. Per-route narrative
### ARG_R1. Ornithine + cyanamide -> arginine
This is the only retained route in the bundle that directly targets free arginine formation from a plausible precursor pair. In the master route table it is classified as a **positive but underconstrained** cyanamide/guanidinylation route, with ornithine and cyanamide as precursors, cyanamide as the key agent, and an **aqueous / concentrating aqueous** medium in a mild surface setting such as an evaporative pond or wet-dry shoreline (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). However, the same row explicitly states that the route is **"Only source-supported as a chemically plausible route; not a dedicated free-arginine prebiotic yield paper. Explicitly marked inferred."** The yield field is **"yield not reported"**, and no exact locator was recovered because the analyze_paper attempt for PMID:32561643 failed with 401 PermissionDenied (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`).

Accordingly, this route should be treated as the bundle's best positive hypothesis, not as a demonstrated prebiotic synthesis with quantified support.

### ARG_R2. Arginine hydrothermal destruction to ornithine
The second strongest route in the retained network is actually degradative. The route table describes **arginine + hot water** under hydrothermal or pyrolytic conditions yielding **ornithine (+ additional products)**, with temperatures discussed across roughly **150–300 C**, and a note that an arginine-specific pyrolysis report identifies primary products at **300 C** (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). The row further notes that this is **"one of the best-supported arginine-specific network edges in the source base: hydrothermal settings act more as sinks than sources."**

This matters for prebiotic plausibility because any environment proposed to synthesize arginine must also overcome its apparent hydrothermal instability in the retained evidence base.

### ARG_R7-R9. Ornithine/citrulline/argininosuccinate pathway analogs
Three rows are retained to preserve the canonical biosynthetic sequence:
- **ARG_R7:** ornithine + carbamoyl phosphate -> citrulline
- **ARG_R8:** citrulline + aspartate -> argininosuccinate
- **ARG_R9:** argininosuccinate -> arginine + fumarate

All three are explicitly labeled **biochemical analog** or **context only**, with aqueous biochemical media, modern metabolic environments, and enzyme catalysts (ornithine transcarbamylase, argininosuccinate synthetase, argininosuccinate lyase) rather than prebiotic catalysts (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). For all three, temperature, pressure, and pH are **not established prebiotically**, and yield is **yield not reported**. These rows are therefore useful as mechanistic scaffolds for precursor logic, but not as evidence of demonstrated abiotic arginine synthesis.

### ARG_R10. Mixed-valence iron oxyhydroxide hydrothermal network
This route was retained because mineral-gradient hydrothermal systems are often invoked in origin-of-life chemistry. In the retained bundle, however, the Barge et al. 2019 *PNAS* system is a **negative scope result** for arginine: products focus on simpler amino acids, while arginine is **not detected** (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). The environment is an aqueous mineral-gradient system with explicit redox and pH gradients and iron oxyhydroxide minerals as the active surfaces. This is therefore direct negative evidence against easy arginine emergence in at least one vent-analog mineral network.

### ARG_R3-R4. Spark-discharge systems
The canonical Miller-Urey route (`ARG_R3`) and the H2S-rich Miller archive variant (`ARG_R4`) are both retained as explicit negative rows. In the canonical case, the route table states that arginine is absent/non-detected in the classic product inventories, despite formation of simpler amino acids (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). In the sulfur-rich Parker et al. 2011 archive reanalysis, **23 amino acids and 4 amines** were reported overall, yet arginine was **not reported among products** (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). These rows are important because they show that even product-rich discharge systems did not recover arginine in the retained evidence base.

### ARG_R5. Ice photolysis
The retained ice-photochemistry row describes low-temperature UV/VUV irradiation of interstellar/cometary ice analogs, followed by hydrolysis/workup. The route is explicitly negative for arginine: multiple simpler amino acids are reported in the source context, but no recovered evidence supports arginine detection (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). This suggests that ice photochemistry, as represented in the retained bundle, is better supported as a source of simpler precursors than of arginine itself.

### ARG_R6. Meteoritic inventory
The meteoritic row is not a reaction step but an occurrence test. It is retained because the task requires explicit treatment of arginine's absence. The route table states that **free arginine is not robustly established in gathered inventories**, and notes that extraction/hydrolysis conditions may affect recovery (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). Thus, the retained meteoritic evidence supports either true absence or unresolved analytical bias, but not a secure positive detection.

## 4. Intermediates and precursors
### Ornithine and citrulline
The retained network places **ornithine** at the center of arginine plausibility. Ornithine appears both as the immediate precursor in the only direct positive-but-underconstrained route (`ARG_R1`) and as the principal daughter product of arginine destruction under hydrothermal conditions (`ARG_R2`) (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). This dual role makes ornithine both a potential precursor and a degradation sink.

**Citrulline** appears only in the biochemical-analogy sequence (`ARG_R7-R9`). The retained bundle therefore supports citrulline as a conceptually relevant intermediate, but not as an experimentally demonstrated prebiotic intermediate under abiotic conditions. The same applies to **argininosuccinate** and **carbamoyl phosphate**: they are retained because they complete the biosynthetic logic, not because the bundle demonstrates their nonenzymatic prebiotic participation (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`; `arginine_s6_summary.json`).

### Guanidine and cyanamide
The retained positive route depends on **cyanamide** as the guanidinylating agent for converting ornithine toward arginine (`ARG_R1`) (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). The s6 issues section also notes that cyanosulfidic/Powell-Sutherland chemistry appears only as contextual support, not as an explicit experimentally demonstrated arginine-producing route row (`arginine_s6_summary.json`). Thus, cyanamide/guanidino chemistry is present as a mechanistic hypothesis, but the bundle does not recover a direct, quantified free-arginine synthesis paper with exact locator.

## 5. Mineral catalysts and surfaces
Only one retained route directly centers mineral surfaces as active participants in an arginine-relevant network: **ARG_R10**, the mixed-valence iron oxyhydroxide hydrothermal system of Barge et al. 2019 (*PNAS*). In that row, the catalyst/surface field is **"iron oxyhydroxide minerals / mixed-valence iron systems"**, the phase is **aqueous mineral-gradient system**, and the environment is **hydrothermal vent analog mineral pores** (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). The outcome, however, is negative for arginine.

Hydrothermal destruction (`ARG_R2`) allows for possible mineral/redox buffering depending on study, but no specific mineral catalyst is reported in the retained route row (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`). No other retained route provides direct mineral-catalyst evidence for productive arginine synthesis.

Therefore, within the retained bundle, mineral surfaces are better supported as part of environments where arginine is absent or unstable than as catalysts that demonstrably produce arginine.

## 6. Experimental evidence and environmental context
The retained experimental contexts span most major prebiotic settings:
- **Mild surface aqueous / wet-dry settings** for the ornithine + cyanamide plausibility route (`ARG_R1`)
- **Hydrothermal aqueous systems** for arginine destruction (`ARG_R2`) and mineral-gradient non-detection (`ARG_R10`)
- **Reducing atmospheric spark discharge** for canonical and sulfur-rich Miller systems (`ARG_R3`, `ARG_R4`)
- **Low-temperature astrochemical ice irradiation** for UV/VUV photolysis (`ARG_R5`)
- **Carbonaceous chondrite parent-body chemistry and delivery** for meteoritic occurrence (`ARG_R6`)

Across these settings, the retained evidence pattern is strikingly asymmetric. Surface aqueous chemistry offers only a plausible, underconstrained positive route with no recovered numeric yield. Hydrothermal settings are represented mainly by destruction or non-detection. Spark-discharge, ice-photolysis, and meteoritic contexts all contribute explicit negative evidence (`arginine_master_route_table.csv`).

### Explicit treatment of arginine's frequently reported absence
The task specifically requires treatment of arginine's frequent absence from abiotic syntheses. In the retained bundle, this absence is supported by five explicit route rows:
1. **Canonical Miller-Urey spark discharge:** arginine absent/non-detected in classic product inventories (`ARG_R3`).
2. **H2S-rich Miller archive discharge:** many amino acids and amines reported overall, but arginine not reported (`ARG_R4`).
3. **Interstellar/cometary ice photolysis:** simpler amino acids reported, arginine not detected (`ARG_R5`).
4. **Meteoritic inventories:** free arginine not robustly established (`ARG_R6`).
5. **Mixed-valence iron oxyhydroxide hydrothermal system:** simpler amino acids emphasized; arginine not reported (`ARG_R10`).

This repeated negative pattern is one of the most robust conclusions available from the retained s6 evidence.

## 7. Open questions and debates
The s6 summary identifies the following unresolved issues, which remain the key debates for arginine:

- **No numeric yield recovered:** No route in the retained bundle has a numeric arginine yield with citation and exact locator (`arginine_s6_summary.json`).
- **Plausibility versus demonstration:** Arginine is chemically plausible via ornithine + cyanamide, but the same bundle lacks a dedicated free-arginine primary yield report for that route (`arginine_s6_summary.json`; `arginine_master_route_table.csv`).
- **Hydrothermal paradox:** Hydrothermal systems are often invoked as generative prebiotic environments, yet in the retained arginine network they appear mainly as destruction or non-detection settings (`arginine_s6_summary.json`; `arginine_master_route_table.csv`).
- **Biochemical analogy gap:** Whether any nonenzymatic ornithine/citrulline/argininosuccinate sequence can operate prebiotically without enzymes remains unresolved (`arginine_s6_summary.json`).
- **Analytical bias versus true absence:** In meteoritic, spark, ice, and mineral-gradient systems, the retained bundle cannot distinguish whether arginine is absent because it never forms, forms but degrades rapidly, or forms below analytical recovery (`arginine_s6_summary.json`).
