## Comparative Analysis
The networks evaluated fall into three distinct tiers. 

**Tier 1 (Configs C, B, and A)** represent expertly crafted, highly plausible networks. **Config C** takes the top spot due to its exceptional novelty; it doesn't just rehash the Miller-Urey and standard Strecker syntheses, but dives deep into state-of-the-art literature (2023-2024), utilizing native metals, acetylene hydrothermals, and proto-biochemical cofactors (pyridoxal) to create a highly advanced network. **Config B** is a close second, offering arguably the most geologically grounded network by perfectly integrating the Sutherland cyanosulfidic model with wet-dry polymerization and chiral selection. **Config A** is similarly excellent and highly comprehensive but slightly less unique than C or B.

**Tier 2 (Config D)** represents a scientifically sound but narrow network. It adheres strictly to a handful of excellent recent papers regarding iron-driven protometabolism but lacks the sprawling redundancy of the top three. Its failure to provide a synthesis route for its primary aminating agent (hydroxylamine) holds it back.

**Tier 3 (Configs E and F)** represent flawed networks. **Config E** attempts complex chemistry but fails on basic mass-balance (e.g., failing to account for the necessary reduction step when transitioning from glycolaldehyde to aminopropionitrile). **Config F** completely fails the prompt, offering a simple linear pathway featuring anachronistic free oxygen and zero environmental context.
