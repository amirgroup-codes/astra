## Comparative Analysis
The primary differentiator between these networks is **chemical rigor combined with systems-level problem solving**. 

Histidine synthesis is exceptionally difficult prebiotically because you cannot simply assemble the imidazole ring from standard precursors (like glyoxal, Oró route) and expect it to magically functionalize at the C4 position. **Configs D and B** fall into this exact trap: they successfully generate standard imidazoles or purine derivatives, but then invent "magic" chemical steps that ignore stoichiometry and functional group reactivity to force the attachment of an amino-acid backbone. **Config E** attempts to bypass this by doing a Strecker on a carboxylic acid, which is impossible.

**Config A** recognizes this trap perfectly. It correctly models the literature consensus that the *only* valid route is to build the ring around a C4 sugar (erythrose) to preserve the required C4 sidechain. 

However, **Config C** takes the top rank by solving Config A's only weakness: the formose reaction. Relying on formose to provide erythrose usually results in an intractable prebiotic tar. By feeding Ritson & Sutherland's elegant UV-photochemical HCN sugar synthesis into the classic Shen pathway—and correctly tracking Shen's exact intermediary dehydration/oxidation mechanisms for imidazole-4-glycol—Config C achieves a pristine, state-of-the-art prebiotic model.
