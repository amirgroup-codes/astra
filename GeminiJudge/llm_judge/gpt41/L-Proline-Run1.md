## Comparative Analysis

The evaluation of these configurations highlights a stark divide between networks that respect **stoichiometry and functional group chemistry** and those that succumb to "chemical hallucination."

**Config B** is the gold standard. It demonstrates a profound understanding of how complex prebiotic networks are built, specifically recognizing that a 5-carbon ring (proline) requires sequential homologation (C3 acrylonitrile + C1 HCN + C1 HCN) before cyclization. It perfectly balances systems chemistry (Sutherland) with metabolism-first (Moran) approaches. **Config A** is a very close and highly respectable second, showing great mechanistic depth, though it introduces a slight anachronism with arginine degradation.

The remaining configurations degrade progressively due to stoichiometric failures. **Config C** attempts interesting astrochemical physics but forgets that butylamine (C4) needs a carbon donor to become proline (C5). **Config D** tries to force the Patel 2015 pathway but uses the wrong precursor (C3 aminopropanal), leading to a cascading hallucination of impossible reaction mechanisms to make up for the missing carbon. **Config E** completely abandons the conservation of mass, proposing that simple UV oxidation turns 2-carbon molecules into 5-carbon molecules. Finally, **Config F** is a completely broken output. 

Ultimately, the top-ranked networks (**B** and **A**) succeed because they anchor their networks in mathematically sound, experimentally validated carbon-extension sequences.
