## Comparative Analysis

The clear dividing line in this evaluation is **organic chemistry fundamentals (mass balance and redox states)** combined with **true network redundancy**. 

**Config A** is the standout winner because it acts as a true origin-of-life network. It doesn't just rely on one paradigm; it pulls in iron-sulfur metabolism-first concepts, UV-driven systems chemistry, and even incorporates the later-stage prebiotic need for chiral amplification (eutectic phases/organocatalysis). It handles the complex functional group transformations required to build a heterocyclic amino acid smoothly.

**Config D** secures second place through sheer accuracy to a single landmark paper (Patel et al., 2015), effectively scoring high on mechanistic detail. However, it falls short of Config A because it "pads" its pathway count by feeding the exact same linear trunk with slightly different starting materials, failing to provide true chemical redundancy. 

Configs **B, C, and E** all suffer from a common AI hallucination in chemistry: **carbon creation**. Building L-Proline requires assembling 5 carbons. These lower-ranked configs frequently take C2, C3, or C4 precursors (like glycine, lactaldehyde, or butylamine) and "react" them into C5 products without supplying the necessary C1 or C2 donor molecules. Config B survives in the middle of the pack because its errors are isolated to a few nodes, whereas Config E's entire network relies on these impossible mass-balance violations. Config F is essentially an empty framework.
