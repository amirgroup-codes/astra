## Comparative Analysis

The evaluated configurations reveal a sharp divide between networks that truly understand prebiotic systems chemistry and those that either oversimplify or hallucinate transformations. 

**Configs C, B, and A** represent the gold standard of origin-of-life modeling. **Config C** edges out the competition by leveraging state-of-the-art research (e.g., native Ni catalysis, ferrocyanide reservoirs), providing exact temperature, pressure, and pH constraints that elevate it from a theoretical map to a reproducible experimental blueprint. **Config B** is a remarkably close second, standing out for its systems-level planetary thinking—incorporating chiral selection on pyrite and exogenous meteorite delivery, which are vital pieces of the prebiotic puzzle often ignored by pure synthetic chemists. **Config A** serves as a highly robust, conservative baseline that correctly maps the canonical textbook pathways.

**Config D** is scientifically rigorous but architecturally limited. By restricting almost the entire network to alpha-keto acid reduction, it lacks the topological redundancy of the top three. 

The bottom two networks fail fundamentally. **Config E** attempts to be complex but violates basic organic chemistry rules (failing to account for the hydroxyl group during a proposed Strecker condensation). Finally, **Config F** acts as an untrained baseline, failing to specify environments and relying on molecular oxygen, a glaring anachronism in prebiotic chemistry.
