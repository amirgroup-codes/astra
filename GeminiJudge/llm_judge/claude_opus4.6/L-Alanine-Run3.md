## Comparative Analysis

The evaluation reveals a massive gulf between simplistic representations of prebiotic chemistry (Config F) and state-of-the-art systems chemistry models (Configs A, B, C, and D). 

The top-ranked configuration (**Config C**) stands out because it explicitly bridges the gap between purely abiotic mineral chemistry and early biochemistry. By utilizing formamide-stabilized nitrile pools (Green 2023) and mineral-charged pyridoxal shuttles (Schlikker 2024 / Dherbassy 2023), it provides a plausible evolutionary trajectory from geochemical to enzymatic metabolism. Furthermore, it treats homochirality realistically—not as a magic byproduct of a synthesis reaction, but as a grueling, iterative physical amplification process (Viedma ripening/CPL).

**Config B** achieved its high rank through a stunning piece of scientific narrative: acknowledging that a proven prebiotic synthesis route (pyrite photocatalysis) actually produces the *wrong* (D-) enantiomer of alanine, and cleverly resolving this paradox by incorporating downstream RNA-templating to select for the L-enantiomer. This demonstrates deep, synthesized domain knowledge. 

**Config D** and **Config A** represent the pinnacle of rigorous "systems chemistry" modeling. Config D meticulously prunes out circular "fatal cycles" (a common error where a network uses a downstream product to synthesize its own upstream precursor), opting instead to pull nitrogen from volcanic NO reduction. Config A provides a masterclass in redundancy.

In contrast, **Config E** feels like a snapshot of origin-of-life research circa 2012—accurate and highly defensible, but lacking the revolutionary discoveries of the Moran and Sutherland groups from the late 2010s/early 2020s. **Config F** serves purely as a baseline representation of a 1950s textbook understanding.
