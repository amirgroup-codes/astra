## Comparative Analysis

The gap between the top-tier configs (D, A, E) and the lower-tier configs (C, B, F) comes down to **chemical grounding versus computational speculation**. 

**Config D** wins because it demonstrates advanced systems-level thinking. By recognizing that complex molecules (serine, threonine, asparagine) decompose into glycine under hydrothermal stress, it models an early Earth where glycine accumulates not just through direct synthesis (bottom-up), but through the degradation of other products (top-down). It also flawlessly patches network "holes" (e.g., generating hydroxylamine from NO).

**Config A** is a close runner-up and represents the best "classic" prebiotic network. It doesn't take the conceptual leaps that D does, but it integrates very modern experimental data (like the 2024 PNAS ferroan brucite study) with an unmatched level of mechanistic accuracy.

Conversely, **Config B** and **Config C** suffer from over-reliance on purely computational chemistry. Config B proposes an SN2 reaction that looks fine on a computer screen but is kinetically highly improbable in a real aqueous surface pool. Config C hallucinates a future 2026 paper to support a high-energy-barrier pathway. 

**Config F** serves as a baseline example of a failure to engage with the systems-chemistry requirements of origin-of-life research.
