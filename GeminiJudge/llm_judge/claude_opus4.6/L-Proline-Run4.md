## Comparative Analysis

The clear dividing line in this evaluation is **how the networks handle known chemical bottlenecks**, specifically the energetic barrier of reducing the $\gamma$-carboxylate of glutamate to an aldehyde (GSA) to form the pyrrolidine ring.

**Config C** wins because it attacks this problem directly like a chemist. Instead of waving a hand and saying "it reduces," it introduces prebiotically plausible activating agents (trimetaphosphate for surface wet-dry cycles; thioesters for hydrothermal vents) to convert the unreactive carboxylate into a reactive acyl-phosphate/thioester prior to reduction. This is an exceptional application of biochemical logic to prebiotic constraints.

**Config B** takes a close second by using architectural creativity. Instead of forcing the difficult reduction, it realizes that GSA could thermally decarboxylate into 4-aminobutanal, which elegantly connects the hydrothermal proto-metabolism network directly into the surface Strecker network. 

**Config A** is the most thorough and well-researched aggregator of existing literature, but it explicitly admits the bottleneck is unsolved rather than innovating a solution. **Config E** provides a highly creative alternative (succinaldehyde), but relies too heavily on the messy formose reaction. **Config D** is highly accurate to literature but functions merely as a transcription of a single paper without adding network robustness. **Config F** serves as a baseline of what a poorly considered, overly lumped network looks like.
