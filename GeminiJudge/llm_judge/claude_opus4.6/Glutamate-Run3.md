## Comparative Analysis

The highest-performing configurations (A, B, and C) distinguished themselves by recognizing that **Glutamate is uniquely difficult to synthesize prebiotically**. Because α-ketoglutarate is the least reactive keto-acid toward reductive amination, a standard approach will fail in a complex mixture. 

**Config A** took the top spot by explicitly addressing this limitation (citing the 2022 Mayer & Moran paradox) and constructing a highly redundant network of *specialized* amination routes (NADH-mediated, H₂/Ni, UV-enantioselective pyrite) to force the reaction forward. It evaluated the literature critically, noting where one-pot synthesis fails and spatial separation is required.

**Config B** achieved near-parity by utilizing brilliant systems-chemistry environmental features. Rather than fighting the thermodynamics of glutamate decomposition, it utilized the spontaneous thermal cyclization of glutamate to pyroglutamate as a protective reservoir, a feature highly relevant to both hydrothermal stability and prebiotic peptide synthesis. 

**Config C** offered the most creative surface-chemistry alternatives, bypassing classical Strecker limitations using DAP and hydantoin chemistry. **Config E** was the most chemically conservative and rigorous regarding the carbon skeleton, but slightly less creative with nitrogen. **Config D** introduced unique top-down degradation routes but lacked completeness. **Config F** failed to execute the basic requirements of the prompt. 

Overall, the best configs didn't just list valid reactions—they used the inherent chemical limitations of the target molecule to inform the architecture and environmental transitions of the network.
