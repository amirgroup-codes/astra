## Comparative Analysis

The clear dividing line in these configurations is how they handle the two hardest problems in prebiotic alanine synthesis: **the aminonitrile hydrolysis bottleneck** and **enantiomeric symmetry breaking (L-homochirality)**.

**Config C** wins because it attacks both problems with high-level, specific chemical physics. It uses formamide-staged heating to create protected *N-formyl* pools to survive the hydrolysis bottleneck, and introduces circularly polarized UV light coupled with chiral mineral adsorption to drive L-enrichment. 

**Configs A and B** are practically tied for a very close second. **Config B** differentiates itself through an incredibly elegant biochemical approach to chirality: it polymerizes racemic alanine into dipeptides (alanylalanine) via wet-dry cycles, allowing kinetic resolution and template effects to naturally select for the L-enantiomer. **Config A** provides the most massive and redundant traditional network, successfully bridging both Bucherer-Bergs and Strecker chemistries.

**Config E** is a highly competent baseline model but lacks the specific, creative solutions to the bottlenecks seen in A, B, and C. **Config D** demonstrates knowledge of niche literature but structurally fails the prompt by assuming complex molecules already exist in the environment. **Config F** failed to execute the prompt's formatting and data requirements entirely.
