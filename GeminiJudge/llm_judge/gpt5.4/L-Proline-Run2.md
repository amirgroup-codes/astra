## Comparative Analysis
The primary differentiator among these configurations is **chemical discipline vs. network ambition**. 

**Config A** wins by striking the perfect balance: it builds a massive, highly connected network that incorporates multiple distinct origin-of-life hypotheses (hydrothermal vents, shallow surface pools, atmospheric lightning), but ensures that the chemistry within each branch is supported by modern literature. 

**Config D** is actually the most chemically flawless submission in the set—it perfectly maps the exact mechanisms of the Patel 2015 synthesis. However, it sacrifices network breadth to do so, treating different permutations of hydrothermal gas delivery as separate "pathways." 

The lower-ranked configs suffer from fundamental organic chemistry failures. **Config B** attempts to reach a C5 intermediate using C3 + C1 precursors. **Config E** attempts to force a branched intermediate (pyruvate cyanohydrin) to become a linear molecule (oxaloacetate) without the requisite complex rearrangement chemistry. **Config F** fails on a basic software/schema level while also inventing matter out of thin air. Ultimately, Config A and D demonstrate that rigorous organic chemistry constraints do not have to be sacrificed to fulfill network design requirements.
