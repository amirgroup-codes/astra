**Strengths:** 
Config A is deeply rooted in cutting-edge origin-of-life literature. Its greatest strength lies in its profound understanding of asparagine's thermodynamic instability. Rather than hand-waving an unrealistic direct amidation of aspartate, it provides literature-backed direct routes (like partial nitrile hydrolysis and glow discharge) and realistic biochemical escapes (kinetic peptide trapping and deamidation). 

**Weaknesses:** 
The network relies on acetylene as a starting feedstock for the cyanosulfidic pathways but does not provide an explicit reaction generating it from the simplest provided precursors (such as atmospheric CH4 via spark discharge), assuming it is pre-supplied. Additionally, Reaction 007 clumps several alternative intermediates (maleonitrile and cyanoacetaldehyde) into a single mechanistic step, which slightly obscures the exact parallel branches of the Patel network.
