**Strengths:** The network is deeply rooted in actual prebiotic chemistry literature, successfully integrating astrochemically relevant PAH photolysis with classic terrestrial discharge chemistry. It acknowledges the inherent difficulty of prebiotic tyrosine synthesis and proposes multiple distinct operational paradigms to solve it. 

**Weaknesses:** The network takes significant chemical shortcuts around the most difficult part of tyrosine synthesis: the hydroxylation of the aromatic ring. Bypassing the mechanism of hydration/oxidation in the phenol-to-aldehyde step, and claiming direct conversion of phenylacetaldehyde to tyrosine without an intermediate hydroxylation step, weakens the chemical feasibility of the terrestrial pathways.
