## Comparative Analysis

The top-ranked network (**Config B**) stands out by successfully bridging the divide between different schools of prebiotic thought. Rather than relying on a single experimental paradigm, it cleanly incorporates Sutherland-style UV-photoredox homologation, classical Strecker chemistry, and Moran-style hydrothermal rTCA pathways into a highly redundant, chemically sound network. Furthermore, it pushes the boundary forward by successfully demonstrating how proline precursors flow into diketopiperazine (peptide) cycling. 

**Config A** attempted a similarly comprehensive approach and scored very high on breadth, but a minor—yet critical—chemical mistake (omitting the amine group on the C4 cyanohydrin precursor) broke the mechanistic chain for its cyanosulfidic pyrrolidine ring closure. 

A clear pattern across the middle configs (**Config C** and **Config D**) is the tension between *empirical accuracy* and *network complexity*. Config D is flawlessly accurate to a specific paper but refuses to build a redundant network. Config C builds a redundant network but relies on macroscopic phenomena (lightning, space dust) rather than explicit chemical mechanisms. 

The lower-ranked configs struggle with chemical reality (**Config E** forces unnatural carbon chain homologations to make its network fit) or structural formatting (**Config F** is incomplete). Overall, Config B is the definitive synthesis, offering the best balance of chemical rigor, pathway redundancy, and environmental plausibility.
