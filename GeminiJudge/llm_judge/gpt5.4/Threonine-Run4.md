## Comparative Analysis

The top-tier configs (**C, A, and D**) stand out because they root their networks in rigorous, experimentally validated literature. **Config C** edges out the competition by functioning almost as an experimental blueprint—it cites specific irradiation wavelengths (254 nm), catalyst concentrations (10 mol % CuCN), and empirical yields (19% lactaldehyde), which is the gold standard for proving prebiotic plausibility. **Config A** is a close second, offering a broader, highly redundant network that smartly routes the fragile threonine into stable peptides. **Config D** takes a unique and highly successful approach by validating the distinct environmental paradigms of the field, proving that hydrothermal vents (Bada's aldol route) and surface pools (Sutherland's cyanosulfidic route) can synergistically exchange feedstocks.

The middle-tier configs (**B and E**) substitute empirical literature validation for creative, theoretical chemistry. **Config B** introduces fascinating but complex pathways like the Weber thioester rearrangement and computational Mg-porphin chemistry. **Config E** attempts a rational "organic synthesis" approach—extending serine to a beta-keto intermediate—which is logically beautiful but lacks the hard experimental backing seen in the top three. 

**Config F** serves as a baseline failure, demonstrating that merely listing stoichiometric reactions without environmental constraints, catalysts, or upstream feedstock generation is insufficient for modern origin-of-life systems chemistry.
