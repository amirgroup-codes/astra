**Strengths:**
- **Literature Integration:** Demonstrates a masterful grasp of origin-of-life literature, accurately citing Patel, Sanchez, Pulletikurti, Harrison, and others.
- **Scientific Honesty:** The explicit acknowledgment of the "asparagine problem" (rapid deamidation, difficulty of partial nitrile hydrolysis, lack of selective aspartate amidation) makes the network highly scientifically rigorous.
- **Systems-Level Thinking:** Including degradation (deamidation) and kinetic trapping (peptide formation) elevates the network from a simple A-to-B map to a realistic model of early Earth chemical dynamics.

**Weaknesses:**
- The categorization of wet-dry cycling and purely inorganic Cu²⁺-catalyzed transamination under the "Biochemical" environment is an awkward fit.
- The direct conversion of CO₂ to pyruvate on FeS minerals is presented as a single hub step, whereas in reality, it is a highly inefficient, contested, and complex multi-step process.
- Atmospheric spark discharge is highly compressed (CO₂ + H₂ + NH₃ → HCN), ignoring the complex radical intermediates required to form the C-N triple bond.
