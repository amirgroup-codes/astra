## Comparative Analysis

The highest-performing networks (**C, A, and D**) succeeded because they prioritized **empirical chemical reality over neat, straight-line biological mimicry**. 

**Config C** stands out as the definitive winner due to its expert-level handling of bench-chemistry realities. Prebiotic chemistry is plagued by kinetic traps and messy side-reactions. Rather than ignoring these, Config C builds them into the network: it acknowledges that Ni-catalyzed reductions produce massive amounts of hydroxyketoglutarate alongside glutamate, and it treats cyanohydrins as a realistic storage reservoir rather than a false productive step. Furthermore, utilizing DAP to bypass the neutral-pH Strecker problem demonstrates a masterful grasp of recent literature.

**Config A** took a different but equally valid approach: encyclopedic breadth. It succeeded by incorporating messy, historical prebiotic chemistry (spark discharges, UV tars) but maintaining scientific rigor by explicitly defining them as low-specificity mixture pools rather than clean, stoichiometric nodes. 

Conversely, **Config E** fell into the classic "metabolism-first" trap. By trying to force pre-biotic chemistry to perfectly trace the modern rTCA cycle, it had to rely on steps that look good on paper but fail in the lab (e.g., non-enzymatic carboxylation of succinate and the accumulation of highly unstable oxaloacetate). **Config F** serves merely as a baseline summary, lacking the systems-level complexity required for origin-of-life modeling.
