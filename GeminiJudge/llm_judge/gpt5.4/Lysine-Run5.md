**Strengths:** 
* Exceptional scientific honesty and adherence to the prebiotic literature; it perfectly captures the "lysine problem" rather than inventing a chemically unjustified fantasy pathway.
* Brilliant inclusion of downstream biochemical/prebiotic selection (montmorillonite adsorption) to explain how trace, low-yield target molecules could still become biologically relevant.
* Effectively bridges diverse experimental paradigms (hydrothermal C1 generation, spark discharge, cyanosulfidic chemistry, astrophysical ice).

**Weaknesses:** 
* Heavy reliance on "black box" macroscopic inventory steps (e.g., spark discharge, UV ice residues) rather than explicit, step-by-step organic reaction mechanisms.
* Several steps are stoichiometrically unbalanced and serve merely as conceptual bridges (e.g., generating C3 acrylonitrile directly from HCN and H₂CO).
* By design, the network does not provide a robust, high-yielding, unified synthesis to the target, instead relying on a tentative single branch alongside mapped failures.
