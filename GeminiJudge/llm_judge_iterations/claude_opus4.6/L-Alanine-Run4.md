## Comparative Analysis
Both configurations propose exceptionally well-researched, state-of-the-art networks that capture the reality of alanine as a highly accessible prebiotic "hub" product. Both correctly identify the need for multi-environment crossover, incorporating Strecker, Bucherer-Bergs, and reductive amination pathways. 

What separates the top-ranked Config 1 is its **structural and environmental rigor**. Config 1 correctly maps high-pressure, hydrogen-rich, mineral-catalyzed reactions strictly to the "Hydrothermal" environment, and UV-driven chemistry to the "Surface." Config 2 suffers from a systematic error in its environmental classification: it assigns purely geochemical processes (like 5 bar H₂ reduction on native nickel, or boiling acid hydrolysis of cyanide polymers) to the "Biochemical" environment. In origin-of-life frameworks, "Biochemical" implies proto-enzymatic or highly organized late-stage cellular-like conditions, not raw high-pressure mineral catalysis. 

However, Config 2 deserves special commendation in the "Novelty" category for creatively incorporating a very recent (2024) discovery regarding pyrite-induced chiral symmetry breaking. Despite this highlight, Config 1's mechanistic perfection and logical consistency make it the definitive superior network.
