## Comparative Analysis
What separates the top-ranked Config 1 from the others is its strict adherence to **geological and environmental constraints**. A major persistent critique of prebiotic chemistry is the "chemist's flask" problem—the assumption that intermediates can magically teleport between incompatible environments (e.g., from a UV-irradiated surface pool down into a high-pressure deep-sea vent). Config 1 explicitly recognizes this hazard and meticulously routes its pathways (like surface-based green rust amination and ZnS photoreduction) to maintain a realistic, one-way geoflow. 

Config 3 arguably has the most elegant *biochemical* topology—its inclusion of the glycine-to-serine aldol condensation and serine-to-pyruvate degradation shows a masterful understanding of systems chemistry and proto-metabolism. However, it falls just short of Config 1 because it falls into the exact spatial transport trap (Surface → Hydrothermal backflow) that Config 1 actively solves. 

Config 2 is a strong, highly diverse alternative that accurately remembers to include atmospheric and exogenous phenomena. The citation of the steam-rich spark discharge producing OH radicals is a fantastic deep-cut from the literature. However, the reliance on impact shock to directly yield the final target feels slightly disconnected from the intricate, stepwise molecular networking that makes Configs 1 and 3 so compelling. All three are expert-level models, reflecting deep engagement with modern origin-of-life literature.
